My name is Sean Burlington, I am a qualified Sea Kayak Leader, based in Bridport.

I have a passion for paddling kayaks on the sea along our magnificent coastline and want to share that passion by helping people develop their skills and just go for a paddle.

When I'm paddling I don't worry - I am in the moment.

It's a form of exercise that I find incredibly helpful in balancing out the amount of time I spend sitting down.

Risk

I would describe myself as a cautious person - but I also belive that many of the risks we face in life are not always obvious and sometimes the worst thing is to risk not doing something.

Kayaking involves some risk, you may well get a blister, sunburn, sore muscles, maybe a bruise, and almost certainly if you kayak on the sea enough - at some point you will get cold and wet.

Some people die kayaking. Not many each year but it does happen: which is why I take safety very seriously to minimise any risk.

Leading

I have gained a Sea Kayak Leader qualification from British Canoeing (the national governing body for paddlesports in the UK) which is an award recognising an ability to lead up to 6 people in the following sea conditions (with some caveats).

A stretch of coastline with some areas where it is not easy to land but there will always be straightforward land points a maximum of two nautical miles apart. Crossings not exceeding two nautical miles. Up to 2 Knots of tide (but not involving tide race or overfalls). Wind strengths do not exceed Beaufort force 4. Launching and landing through surf (up to 1 metre, trough to crest height)..
